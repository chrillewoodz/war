Tutorial

Timer

At the top of the page you will see a timer, when the timer reaches 100% it will change turn to the next player.

Action points (AP)

Each round you start with 20 AP, each action you make (move, deploy, attack, spy, play a card) drains your AP by a certain amount.

When you have reached 0 points you can no longer perform any actions this turn and you will have to press the "End turn" button.

When hovering over an action you will see that the AP text turns to red, this is the AP that would remain after you play the action.

Power value

The red circle with white text is called "power value". It indicates the total power value of an area, or your idle armies of a certain type.

When spying, attacking or defending this value is compared to the one of the enemy of a certain area.

Amount value

The green/blue circle with white text is called "amount value". It indicates the total amount of idle armies of a certain army type.

Seasons

As the game progresses, the seasons will change. The only season that has no effect is spring, indicated by the green leaf.

The rest of the seasons either causes floodings, dehydration or frostbite.

The seasons only affect a certain part of the map, winter for example hits the north, summer the south etc.

Some cards can protect you against these effects.

Selecting areas

Click on an area to select it, then click on a valid connection for this area to select it. You now have a "link" between these two areas, which you can see at the bottom-left of your screen.

Not all areas have their neighbour as a valid connection, for example water and mountains can act as a block between two neighbouring areas.

Deselecting areas / Cancelling

To deselect areas that you have selected, press the "Cancel" button at the bottom left of your screen.

End turn

To end your current turn, click the big red button that says "End turn" at the bottom left of your screen.

Actions

Not all actions require you to first select an area. For example playing certain cards can be played without first selecting an area.

Also, not all actions require you to select two areas. "Deploy" is one such action that only affects the area you selected first.

Spies

With spies you can learn about an enemy area's power value.

You can then use this information to make a decision whether to attack someone.

When a spy has been used, it will remain in that area until the area changes owner.

Be careful however, a spy can be killed during its mission depending on the power value of the area they're infiltrating.

Sending more spies to an area with a high power value will help in making sure the mission was successful.

Army types

There are 3 different armies; soldiers, horses and gatling guns.

They each have their own power value, soldiers have 1, horses 2 and gatling guns have 4.

Selecting armies

When you have chosen an action to perform the army selection menu will pop up.

Use the + and - buttons to set how many armies of that type you want to use for the action.

You can use the "MIN" and "MAX" buttons to quick set a type to 0 or equal to the amount of that army type.

If an army type is opaque like gatling guns is in the image below, it means that you don't have any gatling guns that can be used for the action.

Cards

Cards have a crucial role in the game, they can turn the tide at any given moment.

Each card have an AP cost to them and have a star ranking from 1-3, with 3 being a top-notch card.

Each card have their own requirement in order to be used, read the description to learn how to use the card.

For example, some cards require you to first select an area, and some cards do not.

Some cards are really powerful, so play them carefully...

Game chat

Use the chat to gain useful information about what your enemies might be up to.

Or use it to insult your enemy.

Quit/resign

To quit or resign from a game, open the game settings by clicking the "three lines" button at the bottom right of your screen.

Quitting will leave the game, while resigning will resign from the current game but you can still watch it to the end.
